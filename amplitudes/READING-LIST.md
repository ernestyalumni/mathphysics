### 1. Single-minus gluon tree amplitudes are nonzero
**Authors**: Guevara, Lupsasca, Skinner, Strominger, Weil (on behalf of OpenAI) (2025)  
**arXiv**: [2602.12176v2](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.12176)  
**LaTeX source**: `workspace2/Data/Public/papers/physics/arXiv-2602.12176v2/SMGA.tex`  
**Priority**: **Critical** — this is our benchmark  
**Status**: Actively Reading  
**Our Write-up**: [`08-literature-review.tex` §2](../08-literature-review.tex)  
**Dashboard Tags**: `#single-minus #half-collinear #Klein #discovery #GPT`

**Why it's important**: THE paper to beat. GPT-5.2 Pro conjectured a closed-form formula for single-minus amplitudes in the half-collinear regime. Shows that AI can make genuine physics discoveries in amplitudes. Our goal is to do something similar.

**Key equations**:
- Eq.(16): A_{1...n}|_{R₁} = (1/2^{n-2}) ∏(sg_{m,m+1} + sg_{1,2...m})
- Berends-Giele recursion for SDYM
- Master identity (App. A)
